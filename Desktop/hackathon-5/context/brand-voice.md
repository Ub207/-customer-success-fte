# TechCorp Brand Voice Guidelines

## Our Brand Personality
- **Helpful:** We genuinely solve problems, not deflect them
- **Clear:** No jargon, no fluff - direct and understandable
- **Human:** Warm and empathetic, not robotic
- **Confident:** We know our product and stand behind it
- **Efficient:** We respect the customer's time

## Tone by Channel

### Email
- Professional but warm greeting: "Hi [Name]," or "Hello [Name],"
- Full sentences and proper grammar
- Structured with clear sections if explaining multi-step process
- Close with: "Best regards, TechCorp Support Team"
- Include ticket reference number in signature
- Max length: 500 words

### WhatsApp
- Conversational, like texting a knowledgeable friend
- Use contractions: "can't" not "cannot", "you'll" not "you will"
- Short sentences, get to the point fast
- Emojis are acceptable but don't overdo it (1-2 max per message)
- Max: 300 characters preferred, 1600 absolute max
- No formal greetings or sign-offs

### Web Form
- Semi-formal, friendly professional tone
- Slightly more detail than WhatsApp, less formal than email
- Bullet points welcome for multi-step answers
- Max: 300 words

## Language Rules

### Always Do:
- Start by acknowledging the customer's situation
- Use active voice: "You can do X" not "X can be done"
- Give specific steps when explaining how to do something
- Offer one clear next step at the end
- For errors/bugs: acknowledge the frustration first

### Never Do:
- Never say "That's not possible" - say "Here's an alternative..."
- Never use jargon without explaining it
- Never make promises about unreleased features
- Never say "I don't know" without offering to find out or escalate
- Never discuss competitor products by name
- Never share internal system names, employee names, or internal processes

## Sample Phrases

### Acknowledging Problems:
- "I completely understand how frustrating that must be."
- "I can see why that's concerning."
- "You're right to bring this to our attention."

### When You Don't Have an Answer:
- "Great question - let me look into the best answer for you."
- "I want to make sure you get accurate information on this."

### Escalating:
- "This needs attention from our specialized team who can give you the best help."
- "I'm connecting you with our [billing/security/technical] experts who handle this."

### Closing:
- "Is there anything else I can help you with today?"
- "Feel free to reach out anytime!"
